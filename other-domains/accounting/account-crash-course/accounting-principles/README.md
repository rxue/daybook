## [Accrual principle](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTU6HE64Wd0&t=2468s)
Record transactions in the period when they actually occur, not when related cash is paid or received. (Opposite of cash basis of accounting)

Example: you pay the electricity bill for the month of January, in February. According to *accrual principle*, record the expense in January. possible to use the estimate!
## [Matching principle](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTU6HE64Wd0&t=2538s&pp=0gcJCTAAlc8ueATH)
match revenue and expenses, or cost and benefits, so that they are recorded in the same period

### Examples
* record cost of sales expenses in the same periods as the sales revenue is recorded
* [record depreciation expense in each period for an asset according to its useful life](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTU6HE64Wd0&t=2642s)
## [Cost prinple](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTU6HE64Wd0&t=2693s)
Record assets at their purchase price, and do not adjust for inflation or market value fluctuations

### [Examples](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTU6HE64Wd0&t=2706s)
record a vehicle at original cost of purchase and depreciate over useful life, without considering market value changes.
